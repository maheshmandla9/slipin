# SlipIN — Pitch

*One-page summary. Full detail in [BRD.md](BRD.md) and [PRD.md](PRD.md); this doc distills them for a first read.*

## The one-liner

**Design the person you want to become on a living avatar, feel it by talking to it, and practice being them every day — with visible proof you're changing, in 30 days.**

## The problem

Habit-tracking apps are everywhere. They all optimize *doing* — track the workout, log the water, tick the box. Nobody mainstream tackles *being* — the deeper, harder shift of actually becoming a different kind of person: more confident, calmer under pressure, braver in the room. People who want that (actors prepping a role, self-help readers with no applied tool, students battling shyness, people managing anger or anxiety, folks chasing a goal) are left stitching together books, journals, and willpower with nothing that makes the identity itself feel real day to day.

## The idea

SlipIN turns "become someone" into something you can actually *build and rehearse*:

1. **Design** — dress a 2D avatar in traits and emotions across five zones (beliefs, feelings, voice, actions, habits), either from a ready-made pack or completely free-hand.
2. **Feel it** — talk to the persona you built. It answers in character, so you experience the shift before you commit to it.
3. **Practice it** — a daily loop drawn from real technique (Stanislavski's *Magic If*, Dispenza-style rehearsal, CBT reframing, if-then implementation intentions, the Ben Franklin one-virtue-a-week method): a short morning wear-session, two tiny real-world missions, an evening debrief.
4. **See proof** — every win logs as evidence; a 30-day "then vs. now" report and a shareable persona card make the change visible, to the user and to their friends.

No login, no database — everything lives in the browser. Anonymous by design.

## Who it's for

Actors sharpen role prep. Self-transformation readers finally get an applied tool instead of another book. Students build confidence and focus with gamified streaks. People managing anger or anxiety get a private, non-clinical practice space. Manifestation-minded users get a grounded version — vision plus a real daily action, not just vibes. Gym starters get two weeks of mental prep before the physical work begins.

**One engine, many doors in** — each audience gets its own module and its own landing page, but they're all the same underlying trait/emotion/plan engine.

## What's live today

The MVP is fully built and shipped: the avatar builder, the trait/emotion library, seven original modules plus free-hand, the daily loop, persona chat (split into two modes — *talk to the persona* and a separate *Your Personal Guide* coaching tab), the evidence log, the 30-day report, and full safety/moderation guardrails.

On top of that, a module expansion just shipped: **Celebrity Icons** (train the mindset of real figures — Ronaldo, Kobe, Einstein — via attribute-titled packs, never impersonation), **Movie/TV Characters** and **Anime Heroes** (archetype-based, user personalizes with their own "inspired by" name), and **Mimicry** (the craft of observing and imitating anyone, taught as a skill ladder), plus 11 new Animal Personas and voice/body-language drills folded into Actor Prep.

## Why it can defend its position

The visual metaphor — dress a body in traits, then talk to who you built — is a first-mover hook, but it's copyable. What isn't: the depth of the curated technique library (acting craft + neuroscience-informed rehearsal + CBT + emotional-intensity laddering, all stitched into one coherent daily practice), the evidence-and-proof retention loop, and a deliberately narrow, defensible positioning: **a self-development training tool, never a companion or therapy product.** That last point is a real strategic choice — the companion-chatbot space (Character.AI, Replika) is crowded, legally hot, and explicitly *not* what this is.

## Where it's headed

Validate retention (D7 return, wear-session completion, chat engagement) before spending on the next tier. If it holds: a multi-persona "Playground" for practicing scenes with more than one character, then a Pro-tier live voice scene-partner for actors, then accounts/sync, then monetization — with a formal IP/publicity-rights legal review required before the Celebrity Icons packs go anywhere near a paywall.

## The ask / status

Currently pre-launch: built, tested locally, about to deploy to Vercel. No users yet, no revenue yet. This is a validation-first build — cheap to run (a few dollars a month at low volume), fast to have built (~5 weeks), and designed to prove the core loop works before any bigger investment.
